The verdict: skill only counts versus the base rate

A raw hit rate lies. Over 3,254 player-rounds this tournament, any featured player scored ≥5 points 21% of the time (the bar a "buy" or "captain" call must clear) and ≤2 points 68% of the time (the bar a "sell / won't feature" call clears by default). So each call is judged against how often anyone would have been right by chance — the black line.

The retro exposed a bug in the feedback loop

The bot graded its own calls live, before FIFA finished settling points — and never re-scored. So its self-assessment was ~7 points too generous, and it recorded Vinícius's 15-point captain haul as a miss. The "learn which sources to trust" loop was training on partly-wrong labels.

What would make the next bot smarter

The data points at a clear thesis — trust the deterministic model over the LLM exactly where the LLM is weakest — and at concrete fixes.

Grade on lift over chance, not raw hit/miss

Reward beating the base rate, not just being right. This instantly stops crediting "sell" calls for predicting the obvious (blanks are 68% likely) and surfaces that captaincy is underwater. Store per-stage base rates and score the delta.

Fix the settlement race before trusting the loop

Re-score after FIFA fully settles (a known ~15–35 min lag), or grade off the leak-proof round-scoped team/history endpoint. A trust-weighting flywheel is only as good as its labels.

Hand captaincy to the deterministic model

Availability (deterministic) was the one clear win; captaincy (LLM) the one clear loss. Route the armband to the Quant/Poisson projection × fixture difficulty and let the LLM only veto, never pick. Score it as a ranking problem — did the C finish top of your XI? — not a ≥5 threshold.

Prune or truly diversify the council

Three interchangeable free models at 43–50% bought agreement, not coverage. Either keep the one with proven lift, or deliberately assign opposing stances (chalk vs contrarian/differential) so the ensemble spans the space instead of nodding along.

Let the head ignore negative-lift sources

The infrastructure to weight advisors by track record already exists — make it base-rate-adjusted and allow a weight of zero. A source with negative lift (the captaincy LLM) should be dropped, not averaged in.

Log structured picks, and grade the head itself

101 calls were regex-scraped from prose, with noise. Have advisors emit structured JSON picks, and log the head's synthesized verdict as gradeable predictions too — so next time we can answer the real question: did synthesis beat its raw inputs?
